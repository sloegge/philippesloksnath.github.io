I am a Research and Teaching Assistant at the Department of Communication and Media Research, supervised by Tobias Dienlin at University of Zurich. Previously, I studied psychology at the University of Zurich. My research is situated in Communication Science, Psychology and Human–Computer Interaction. I care about statistical inference and transparent, reproducible research practices.

Feel free to reach out — I’m always happy to chat about research, collaboration, or anything else. Email: p.sloksnath[at]ikmz.uzh.ch.

I am an Editor for DOCA - Database of Variables for Content Analysis. The open access database compiles, systematizes, and evaluates relevant content-analytical variables of communication and political science research areas and topics. It serves as a foundation for answering questions about research designs and operationalizations resorting to content analysis and helps standardize and compare studies. Here is the link for our Call for Submissions.

I am interested in understanding how AI systems influence human beliefs, attitudes and behavior, how this may impact our society and how AI-based persuasion relates to perceived autonomy. In particular, my research interests include:

  • AI-based persuasion: I study how AI-generated content shapes attitudes and beliefs, and what makes AI-produced messages more or less persuasive than human-authored ones.

  • Misinformation: I examine how we can improve people’s detection of false or misleading information, and how it affects people’s beliefs and decisions.

  • Human–computer interaction: I explore how people engage with and form mental models of AI systems, including the social and psychological consequences of interacting with AI in everyday contexts.

  • Open science: I value scientific integrity and strive to make my research as reliable, transparent, and reproducible as possible. Please reach out if anything is unclear, insufficiently reported, or not reproducible.
